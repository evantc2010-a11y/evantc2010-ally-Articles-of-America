Andy Burnham is UK's Next Prime Minister

By Evan Chen · July 20, 2026

Key Points

  • he is now the seventh prime minister in a decade

The former mayor of Manchester, Andy Burnham, is now the United Kingdom’s prime minister following his meeting with King Charles III. He later addressed the British public outside 10 Downing Street.

During his speech, he promised to make politics work and to bring forward a ten‑year plan for the UK.

Keir Starmer also spoke briefly, saying “My work is done” and offering “full support” to his successor.

Burnham will take over from Starmer, who first came to power in a landslide election victory in July 2024, ending 14 years of Conservative rule. Throughout his premiership, Starmer faced multiple controversies.

His ambassador to the U.S., Peter Mandelson, was mired in accusations over ties to disgraced financier Jeffrey Epstein. It was revealed that Mandelson had failed security vetting yet was still appointed to the position, leading to the resignation of Starmer’s chief of staff, a collapse in Starmer’s approval ratings, and a disastrous showing for Labour in the May 2025 elections.

The following year brought little improvement. The May 6, 2026 elections saw another historic loss for Labour, with 1,500 council seats lost. Reform UK emerged as the dominant political force, sweeping 1,450 council seats in its strongest performance yet. Starmer faced calls to resign from lawmakers of his own party such as Clive Lewis, per NPR, but insisted he would not walk away and “plunge the country into chaos.”

That position did not hold. Under immense pressure from his own party, and following the May 16 March on London led by British activist Tommy Robinson, Starmer announced his formal resignation on June 22, 2026—46 days after the election.

Per CBS News, Burnham has made previous comments about President Trump, and they have not been positive ones.

He stated on the campaign trail that Americans were living in a “polarized poisonous politics where people in communities don’t work together anymore.” During the January 6 attack on the Capitol, he tweeted, “Any UK politician who gave Trump the time of day should be ashamed right now.”

He also compared President Trump’s “instability” to that of former prime minister Liz Truss, saying, “the instability that Liz Truss brought to Britain, I think Trump is bringing to the U.S. and the world.”

The White House was asked by CBS about these comments, and Deputy Press Secretary Anna Kelly responded:

“Left-wing policies of unfettered migration and destructive globalism have made once-great European cities unrecognizable – allowing criminals to run wild and antisemitism to thrive. President Trump saved the United States from such destruction when he was elected, and he has rightfully warned other world leaders that western civilization will continue to erode if they don't quickly reverse course."

The president and Prime Minister Burnham have yet to announce a meeting.
