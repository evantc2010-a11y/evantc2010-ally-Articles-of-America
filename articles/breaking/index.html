US cuts all trade with NATO ally Spain

By Evan Chen · July 08, 2026

Key Points

  • Trump demands END to Spanish Trade

During a bilateral meeting between President Trump and NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organization) Secretary-General Mark Rutte, President Trump announced that he will cut off all trade with NATO ally Spain.

This occurred during the yearly NATO summit, held this time in Ankara, Türkiye. According to NATO, the purpose of this summit is to affirm that allies are investing more into the alliance, demonstrate how NATO’s defense industry is creating stronger equipment and supply chains, and build on NATO’s support for Ukraine in its war against Russia, now entering its fifth year.

The president’s remarks included calling Spain a “lost cause” and “a terrible partner” of NATO. Secretary Rutte later added that President Trump did get Spain to pay 2%, but that there are still “issues we have to solve.”

This is not the first time Trump has criticized Spain. When Prime Minister Sanchez Pedro blocked the U.S. from using the Rota and Moron Air Bases to launch bombing campaigns on Iran in 2026, Trump told Treasury Secretary Bessent to “cut off all dealing with Spain” during a meeting at the White House with the German Chancellor. He also suggested the idea of expelling Spain entirely from NATO during a meeting with President Stubb of Finland in 2025, saying “Maybe you should throw them out of NATO, frankly.”

For context, Spain is the only country in NATO to opt out of NATO’s 5 percent agreement signed on June 25, 2025. As of 2026, Spain has contributed only 2.0% of its GDP to NATO, which translates to approximately 40.2 billion U.S. dollars or 35.26 billion euros. As for trade, the U.S. holds a trade surplus with Spain, meaning that the United States exports more goods to Spain than it imports.

Regarding how this could impact Spain, the country is closely connected with the E.U. (European Union). This means its trade policies are strictly bound to the EU. If there were an embargo on Spain, it would affect all other member states, creating a ripple effect across much of Europe.

Prime Minister Pedro has commented on this, per Reuters. His office says that Trump’s demands are being treated as “business as usual,” and a spokesman for Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez said the country enjoyed excellent social, cultural, and economic relations with the U.S., as reported by the Telegraph.

President Trump has also held bilateral meetings with the President of Syria and Volodymyr Zelensky of Ukraine.
