Before I say anything else, I want to acknowledge the concerns many people have about today's media. A recent Gallup poll found that 28% of Americans have little to no trust in the mainstream media. I understand those concerns because I've seen the flaws myself, and they inspired me to try to make a difference.

Growing up, I was always fascinated with the world around me. My first exposure to the media began with watching NBC Nightly News. At the same time, my history teachers at Middle School instilled a deep appreciation for my country that will cling to me forever. They brought history to life, and taught me that our short history was more complex and meaningful than ever before. It was from them that I knew that somehow, I would find a way to keep the nation just as my teachers admired it as: A place with freedom, prosperity and a strong press.

AsI grew older and became more interested in government and civic matters, I began to notice problems within the media I had admired for so long. There was a growing loss of common sense within them, they were detached from the people and aloof. 

That is why I founded Articles of America. My goal is simple: To provide honest, thoughtful journalism that promotes civic participation and keeps the public informed. We don’t just ask questions, we ask the people’s questions. It’s why our motto is “Always There” because we will always be there for those events, for you and your family. Sometimes, I don’t even consider myself a journalist, I’m a fighter. Fighting for America’s present and future. 

We broadcast news from around the world but are mainly focused on the US and the West, seeking understanding, facts, and accountability. You will be seeing uncensored live coverage of special events with no analysis so you can make up your own judgement without influence.
