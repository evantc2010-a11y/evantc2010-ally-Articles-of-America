BREAKING: Graham Platner announces he will drop out of the race for U.S. Senate following Bombshell Allegations of rape

By Evan chen · July 09, 2026

Key Points

  • BREAKING: Planter OUT as Senate Democrat nominee

Following mounting backlash from both parties, Maine Senate Democratic nominee Graham Planter announced on X that he will suspend his campaign and file paperwork to withdraw from the race—just two days after Politico published explosive allegations against him.

As of a few minutes ago, Planter had been challenging Republican Senator Susan Collins and recently defeated Janet Mills in the June 9 primary.

In an 11‑minute video posted to his X page, Planter called the decision “incredibly difficult” but insisted it was not “an admission of guilt.” He claimed he was stepping aside because of “the structures that are being taken away from us by those in power.” Planter again denied the rape allegations, again calling then false and said they were not his reason for dropping out.

Pressure on Planter intensified after Politico reported that a woman who previously dated him alleged he raped her. Shortly after the report, Planter released a video denouncing the allegations as smears and untrue.

CNN’s Jake Tapper later interviewed the woman, Jenny Racicot. Racicot alleged that in 2021, Planter arrived at her home in Massachusetts and raped her on the couch. She said he was intoxicated and not listening to her, describing a sewing kit spilling during the encounter and pushing him away with her legs.

Racicot said she waited to come forward because of a “huge moral conflict” between her support for Planter’s policies and her feelings about him personally. She said she now wants people to have “the full scope of who he is as a person.”

Senator Bernie Sanders of Vermont recently joined the growing list of high‑profile Democrats urging Planter to withdraw. Others include Congressman Ro Khanna, who rescinded his support on July 6; Senator Elizabeth Warren on July 7; and the Maine Democratic Party leadership, also on July 6.

Planter’s withdrawal comes five days before the July 13 deadline, after which Maine Democrats would have been unable to replace him on the general election ballot.

It remains unclear who will replace Planter. According to CBS News, notable potential candidates include Maine Secretary of State Shannon Bellows, former State Senate President Troy Jackson, and former Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention Director Nirav Shah, all of whom have expressed interest in the seat.
